## What The Manifest Actually Says

- `present` families are usable now, but some still carry `index` or `lazy` hints.
- `partial` families are real but incomplete; missing roots must stay visible.
- `missing` families are blocked procurement lanes; do not synthesize payloads.

## Practical Reading Notes

- Treat `target paths` as the actual roots in the manifest; some families target directories and others target concrete files.
- Preserve load hints: `preload`, `index`, and `lazy` describe how the current registry expects each family to be consumed.
- Use the example field/record type as the downstream operator card shape, not as a claim that every payload is fully materialized.

## Global Cautions

- Interaction-network families are the largest current blocker: string, biogrid, and intact remain missing in the local registry.
- Motif families are still blocked: prosite, elm, mega_motif_base, and motivated_proteins are missing.
- Q9UCM0 remains the cleanest sequence-only gap; do not infer extra modalities from the packet.
- P69905 is the strongest multimodal anchor, but motif rows remain index-only because the motif family is still missing.
- Derived-training and release-artifact families are support layers; do not confuse them with primary biological evidence.
