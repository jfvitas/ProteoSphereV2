# Source Compatibility Matrix

Derived from the completed source reports in `docs/reports/` and the current procurement outputs under `execution/acquire/`. This matrix is intentionally implementation-grade: it separates identity backbone sources, structure layers, motif layers, interaction layers, and pathway/context layers, and it marks where the repository already has a procurement module versus where the source is still report-only.

## Compatibility Rules

- UniProt is the canonical protein join for almost everything else. If a source is protein-bearing, join it to UniProt first and preserve secondary accessions as aliases, not as primary identity.
- RCSB/PDBe and AlphaFold DB are complementary structure layers. RCSB/PDBe is experimental truth; AlphaFold DB is predicted coverage. Keep them separate and never merge predicted and experimental coordinates into one object.
- BioGRID and IntAct are complementary interaction layers. BioGRID is broader and includes genetic interactions; IntAct is deeper on curated PSI-MI evidence and native complexes. Preserve the interaction type and projection lineage.
- InterPro, PROSITE, ELM, and RCSB motif search are complementary motif layers. InterPro is the umbrella spine, PROSITE is the precise sequence-site layer, ELM captures short linear motifs with partner context, and RCSB motif search retrieves structure-linked motifs.
- DisProt is a disorder context source, not an identity backbone and not a negative-label source. Absence of DisProt annotation is not evidence of order.
- Reactome is pathway/reaction context, not a replacement for structure, assay, or identity sources. It is most useful as a grouping and weak-label layer.
- EMDB is a map-level modality overlay. Use it to enrich already anchored protein or complex examples, not as the primary record.
- Evolutionary and MSA sources should drive redundancy reduction, conservation weighting, and family-aware splits. They are priors, not direct functional truth.

## Main Conflicts To Preserve

- Do not collapse AlphaFold predicted structure into RCSB/PDBe experimental structure.
- Do not infer negative disorder labels from a lack of DisProt coverage.
- Do not flatten IntAct native complexes into binary edges without keeping the expansion flag or lineage.
- Do not treat BioGRID physical and genetic interactions as interchangeable evidence.
- Do not use Reactome inferred human events as if they were always direct human experimental evidence.
- Do not trust BindingDB target sequence text as a perfect proxy for the assayed construct; keep strict mapping and provenance.

## Implementation Posture

The practical storage split is:

- Preload: UniProt identity summaries, RCSB/PDBe headers, BindingDB interaction summaries, BioGRID/IntAct interaction summaries, Reactome hierarchy, InterPro/PROSITE/ELM accession catalogs, and compact conservation summaries.
- Index: all accession namespaces, release stamps, taxon IDs, chain/entity IDs, residue spans, and source-specific evidence flags.
- Lazy fetch: coordinate files, validation reports, full motif tables, full interaction rows, pathway diagrams, raw map files, long textual detail pages, and alignment blocks.

That split is the safest way to keep the next hardened integration step narrow: the matrix should drive selection and packaging, not broad source mirroring.
