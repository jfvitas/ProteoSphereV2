# p79 Ligand Queue Truth Review

This review checks whether the new ligand queue card is truthful and where wording could drift into overclaiming.

## Verdict

The queue card is truthful.

It is consistent with the ligand identity pilot preview, the support-ready readiness surface, and the live operator dashboard. It stays report-only, does not materialize ligand rows, and keeps `Q9UCM0` deferred.

## Why It Is Truthful

- The queue preview and the support surface agree on the same four ordered accessions.
- `P00387` is the lead anchor in both artifacts.
- `Q9NZD4` is the only rescuable-now lane in both artifacts, and the local evidence ties it to a concrete bridge at `1Y01`.
- `P09105` and `Q2TAC2` remain hold-for-acquisition lanes.
- `Q9UCM0` remains deferred in every grounding artifact.
- The operator dashboard is still `no-go`, which keeps the queue in preview mode only.

## Wording Risks

- Do not describe the queue as a materialized ligand surface. It is only a preview queue.
- Do not treat `P00387` first as proof that a ligand payload already exists. It only marks implementation priority.
- Do not summarize `Q9NZD4` as a generic rescue without mentioning the `1Y01` bridge.
- Do not collapse `P09105` and `Q2TAC2` into hard blockers. They are hold-for-acquisition lanes.
- Do not drop `Q9UCM0` from summaries. It must remain visible as deferred.

## Summarization Risks

- Collapsing the four ordered rows into a single "ligand pilot" label would erase the difference between actionable, rescuable-now, and hold-for-acquisition lanes.
- Calling the queue a materialization step would overclaim beyond the live bundle state.
- Omitting the deferred accession would hide the current truth boundary.

## Recommended Operator Summary

The safest summary is:

`P00387` first, `Q9NZD4` second, `P09105` and `Q2TAC2` as hold-for-acquisition follow-ups, and `Q9UCM0` deferred.

## Bottom Line

The ligand queue card is truthful as a report-only operator preview, but it should never be summarized as a completed ligand surface or a bundle mutation.
